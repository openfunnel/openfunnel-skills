# Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)


## What Makes Up an Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

An ICP is a **static definition** of who your ideal customer is. It describes the characteristics of companies that are the best fit for your product — it does not change day to day.

**ICP = Firmographic Fit + Trait Fit**

### Firmographic Fit (Who could buy)
The table-stakes filter. Industry, employee count, funding stage, revenue range, geography, tech stack.
- "B2B SaaS companies, 100–1000 employees, Series A+, US-based"
- This is what every database sells. It narrows the universe but tells you nothing about timing or intent.
- Firmographic fit alone produces massive lists where 95%+ have zero interest right now.

### Trait Fit (Who should buy)
Specific company characteristics that indicate product-market alignment beyond firmographics.
- "Companies with a developer documentation portal" or "companies that sell to enterprises and have <3 star Trustpilot ratings"
- Traits describe what a company IS — they change slowly.
- Trait search = finding companies by describing their characteristics in natural language.
- Traits can be mass-blasted but lack timing, budget, and context for outreach. If your product doesn't need timing, trait-based outreach can work.

### ICP is static. OpenFunnel adds the dynamic layers.

An ICP tells you WHO to target. It does not tell you WHEN to reach out or WHY now. That's the gap. OpenFunnel specializes in three offerings on top of a static ICP:

- **ICP + Pain-Points** — finding companies in your ICP that have observable, active pain your product solves
- **ICP + Buying Windows** — finding companies in your ICP that are in a buying window right now (budget secured, mandate given, timeline active)
- **ICP + Signals** — finding companies in your ICP that are exhibiting real-time activities indicating need, intent, or readiness

These are not separate products — they're lenses on the same underlying capability: starting from a static ICP definition and layering dynamic, time-sensitive intelligence to surface who is ready to buy right now.

### How these layers work

**Pain-Points (Active need):**
Observable evidence that a company is experiencing a problem your product solves.
- "CTO posting about migration challenges" or "support team scaling after quality complaints"
- Pain-points are the WHY — the reason a company would buy.
- Without active pain, outreach is a guess. With it, you have a reason code.

**Buying Windows (Active timing):**
The period when a company has budget, mandate, and decision-maker alignment to purchase.
- Relevant job posted → person hired (2-month window). New exec with 90-day pressure to pick vendors.
- A company can have pain but no budget, or budget but no urgency. Buying windows are where all three converge.
- Buying windows are perishable. A signal from 3 months ago is stale. A signal from this week is hot.

**Signals (Active behavior):**
Observable company activities — hiring patterns, social engagement, tech changes, competitive moves — that indicate intent or readiness.
- "Hiring 3 ML engineers after raising Series B" or "ICP decision-makers engaging with competitor content"
- Signals describe what a company DOES — they change rapidly and are time-sensitive.
- Signals are the raw evidence. Pain-points and buying windows are interpretations of signals.

### How it all fits together

- ICP alone = static database (Apollo, ZoomInfo) — everyone has this
- ICP + Signals = signal-qualified prospect — knows there's active behavior
- ICP + Pain-Points = pain-qualified prospect — knows there's active need
- ICP + Buying Windows = timing-qualified prospect — right company, right pain, right moment

**The interplay:**
- "Signals show what a prospect is doing; traits describe who the prospect is."
- ICP is the filter. Signals are the evidence. Pain-points are the reason. Buying windows are the timing.
- Best results come from: ICP-qualified companies + Signal-detected pain + Buying window timing + People-mapped contacts.
- ICP narrows the universe. Signals tell you when to act. People tell you who to contact.

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) Modeling

An ICP is a static definition: Firmographic Fit + Trait Fit. It describes WHO your ideal customer is — it doesn't change day to day.

### Defining an ICP

**ICP = Firmographic Fit + Trait Fit:**
- Firmographic fit (baseline): industry, size, funding, location, tech stack
- Trait fit: specific characteristics that indicate product-market alignment beyond firmographics

Signal fit and timing fit are NOT part of the ICP — they are dynamic layers that OpenFunnel adds on top to find active buying windows within an ICP.

**ICP Qualified + Signal Active = Actionable Lead**

### Lookalike Models

"X is a believable proof-point for Y" — a lookalike isn't just "similar to X," it's "Y buys because X bought."

- Lookalike discovery starts from traits of best customers, then finds companies with similar traits AND active signals.
- Tier-1 lookalikes combine trait similarity with real-time signal activity.
- Lookalike segments can have different signal relevance — what worked for customer A may not be the same signal that works for customer B's lookalike.
