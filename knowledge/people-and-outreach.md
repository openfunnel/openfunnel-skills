# People-First Approach, Micro-Audiences & Evidence-Based Outbound


## People-First Approach

"The whole product is about people. Not accounts, not companies. People."

### Why People > Accounts

- Every buying window is also a person:
  - Hiring/hired → find the person who just got hired to solve the problem you solve
  - Build-out → find the person leading the build-out (hiring manager posting 4 roles)
  - First hire → find the person who is the first hire (they are the buyer)
- Account-level signals are meaningless without knowing WHO to talk to about them
- LinkedIn titles alone aren't helpful in finding the right people — you need context about their role in the buying decision

### The Unified People Pitch

"Find ICP people at ICP-trait companies doing something relevant right now that indicates they're about to buy."

The atomic unit is always: **Person + Date + Context.**

- At the base tier, context comes from individual signals — social activity, competitor engagement, hiring activity, company socials. Each signal gives reps a reason to reach out that isn't cold.
- Many people can share the same context. If the signal is "Company X is building out DevOps," the hiring manager, first hire, and VP above them all share that context. Three people, one story, one moment.
- The output: people, grouped by context, sorted by date.
- The upgrade from base to buying windows: Person + Date + Context where context upgrades from individual signals to temporal patterns across multiple events.

### People Mapping

- **Signal-to-People:** Every signal should map to specific people — who posted the job, who got hired, who engaged with the content. Signal without people = account intelligence without actionability.
- **ICP Roles:** Target roles that are most likely involved in the buying decision for the specific signal. Ranking of ICP roles when multiple people share the same role becomes mission-critical.
- **Team & Department:** Understanding which team/department the signal relates to determines which people matter. Team and product-line splitting is not just good-to-have but mission-critical for accurate people matching.
- **Seniority Matching:** Different signals warrant reaching different seniority levels.
- **People Activity & Trait Search:** Beyond static database matching — find people based on what they're doing (LinkedIn activity, content engagement, job changes), not just their title.
- **People Finder Agents:** Analyze titles, traits, industry context, company size, team structures, and LinkedIn interaction patterns to identify the right contacts. Goes beyond "find me the VP of Engineering" to "find me the person leading the infrastructure migration."

## Micro-Audiences

A micro-audience is a highly targeted, signal-qualified segment — not a mass list.

- Built by combining trait search + signal activity + research-based qualification.
- Research agents split audiences and create sub-segments based on different buying reasons.
- Each micro-audience has its own messaging angle based on the specific signal that qualified them.
- Micro-audiences are continuously refreshed as new signals emerge — they're living segments, not static lists.

**Key insight:** "Spray and pray" is dead. Micro-audiences with signal-rich context convert because the outreach is relevant to what the company is actually doing.

## Evidence-Based Outbound

Every outreach should be backed by observable evidence, not generic personalization.

### What Evidence-Based Means

- **Evidence Link:** A URL to the actual signal source (job posting, LinkedIn post, SEC filing, tech adoption announcement).
- **Reason Code:** A human-readable explanation of why this account was surfaced.
- **Evidence Snippet:** The specific text or data point from the source that indicates intent.

### Why It Matters

- "Personalization" in most outbound = "I see you're the VP of Sales at {company}" — this is not personalization, it's mail merge.
- Evidence-based outbound = "I noticed your team just posted 3 ML engineering roles focused on self-hosted LLM infrastructure — are you evaluating orchestration tools?"
- The prospect can verify the claim. It shows genuine research. It's relevant to what they're actually doing.

**Key principle:** "Let your prospects thank you for reaching out to them" — outreach should feel like a useful heads-up, not spam.

## The Outreach Problem & Nurture-Based Selling

### Why Outreach is Failing

- "Email is a dead channel due to AI-SDR spamming the world at scale."
- Cold outbound without timing or context is noise. The channel is dying because everyone is doing it badly.
- "Outbound relevance & timing >>> Personalization" — the problem isn't the email template, it's reaching the wrong people at the wrong time.
- "Your automated sequencing is wasting everyone's time." Direct outbound sequences are dead.
- You cannot expect scaled outbound to work — especially if you are in B2B selling SaaS or services. You are competing for attention, better value provision, and messaging than your competitors.

### Nurture-Based Selling

The replacement for sequence-based spam is nurture-based selling — every follow-up should provide value:

1. First message: pure value (insight, relevant signal context)
2. Second message: case study (social proof from similar company)
3. Third follow-up: dinner/event invite (relationship building)
4. Fourth follow-up: engage with their content (reciprocity)
5. Fifth follow-up: view their posts (visibility)
6. Sixth follow-up: free credits/value for the audience they belong to

**Core principle:** "You HAVE to be focused that you are reaching out to customers who have a pain point and are willing to talk."

Step 1 is getting attention, trust, and brand. Step 2 is providing value. Only then does selling begin.

### Signal-Based Outreach

- Message should reference the specific signal: "I noticed your team just posted for X..."
- Evidence URL included so prospect can verify the relevance.
- Different signals warrant different messaging angles and channels.
- LinkedIn as primary channel (higher response rates than email for signal-based outreach).
- "Thinking about outreach — it is a dying channel — what are we building towards?" → The answer is relevance through signals.
- The "LinkedIn Likes Effect without having LinkedIn Likes" — achieving the warm-touch visibility that social engagement provides, but through signal-based relevance instead.

### The Signal-to-Message Pipeline

1. Signal detected → reason code generated
2. Research agent adds context (company summary, competitive landscape, recent news)
3. Message builder creates channel-specific copy (LinkedIn connect, email, ad)
4. Rep reviews and sends with one click — or auto-sends for high-confidence signals
5. Context agents maintain ambient memory of accounts, evidence links, and past decisions — enabling outreach that references specific signals without re-prompting
