# External World Events as Inbound

## The Idea

External world events become a second inbound channel — domain-specific events detected by agents, delivered as net-new accounts in the SDR's queue, same workflow as form fills. Not "better than inbound" — it IS inbound, from a source that's never existed before.

Today, inbound means a prospect filled out a form, downloaded content, or requested a demo. That's the only way net-new accounts appear in an SDR's queue without outbound effort.

OpenFunnel creates a second inbound channel: external world events that are specific to what your customers care about, detected automatically, and delivered as ready-to-work accounts — the same way a form fill lands in a queue today.

## How It Works

1. Customer defines their domain — what pain they solve, who they sell to, what signals matter
2. OpenFunnel agents continuously scan external events: job postings, social posts, hiring surges, leadership changes, tech adoption, public announcements
3. Events that match the customer's domain get captured as net-new accounts with context
4. These accounts route directly to SDRs — same as inbound leads, but sourced from the external world

The SDR's experience is identical to working inbound: accounts show up in their queue, enriched with context, ready to act on. The difference is where they came from — not a form fill, but a real-world event that signals active pain.

## Why This Is New

**Inbound today:** Prospect self-identifies by raising their hand. You wait for them to come to you.

**External event inbound:** The world tells you a company is in pain — through their actions, not through your form. You don't wait. The account appears in your queue because something happened in the world that's relevant to what you sell.

| | Traditional Inbound | External Event Inbound |
|---|---|---|
| **Source** | Prospect fills out a form | Company posts a job, makes an announcement, hires a leader |
| **Who initiates** | Prospect | The world — detected by agents |
| **Signal type** | Self-reported interest | Involuntary, behavioral, domain-specific |
| **Timing** | Late — they're already evaluating | Early — before they've built a shortlist |
| **Volume** | Limited by your marketing reach | Limited by how much is happening in your domain |
| **Net-new accounts** | Only people who know you exist | Companies that may never have heard of you |

## What Changes

- **SDRs get a new queue** — not just form fills, but external events that match their domain. Same workflow, different source.
- **Net-new accounts that never would have come inbound** — companies in active pain that don't know you exist yet. They didn't fill out your form because they don't know about you. But they posted a job that describes the problem you solve.
- **Timing shifts from reactive to proactive** — you're not waiting for them to self-identify. You're there when the event happens.
- **The inbound/outbound line blurs** — these accounts arrive like inbound (they show up in your queue) but you're reaching out like outbound (they didn't ask to hear from you). The difference: you have evidence of pain before you reach out. That's what makes it work.
