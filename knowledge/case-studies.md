# Case Studies & The Search Engine for GTM


## Case Studies: Signal-Driven GTM in Practice

### Cekura — Voice AI Vertical, 50% Conversion Rate
- **Problem:** Cekura (YC, automated QA for Voice AI agents) needed to find enterprise companies rolling out Voice AI — a niche vertical where static databases had zero coverage.
- **Solution:** Deployed ~30+ reasoning-based signals on auto-pilot daily. Agents scan job surges, hires, partnerships, and social chatter for custom cues like "scaling Voice-AI engineering team" and "adding Voice Agent capabilities to Call Centers."
- **Signal stacking:** Multiple corroborating signals combine and rank accounts by signal density, surfacing highest-value targets first.
- **Result:** Entered sales motion with 5 of 10 high-ACV Voice AI accounts in first month, all discovered through OpenFunnel signals. "Half the targets booked calls within weeks" — 50% conversion rate.
- **Key takeaway:** Vertical-specific micro-audiences + signal density ranking + hyper-precise messaging = enterprise conversion at startup speed.

### Central — Funding Signals Before They Hit the News
- **Problem:** Central relied on slow funding databases (Crunchbase, PitchBook) that surfaced rounds days after founders had already announced on LinkedIn/X, putting them behind competitors.
- **Solution:** OpenFunnel deploys real-time listeners for SEC Form D filings (capturing funding events months ahead of static databases), monitors socials for batch acceptance announcements (YC, a16z, Sequoia Arc, Pear VC), and auto-finds founder LinkedIn profiles and emails.
- **Signal stacking:** Funding signals + founder contact enrichment + evidence-backed messaging hooks, synced directly to HubSpot.
- **Result:** Real-time, high-confidence alerts replace slow databases and manual tab-hopping. Central builds latest-batch micro-audiences and launches sniper campaigns the same week with verified contacts and evidence-backed hooks.
- **Key takeaway:** Speed-to-signal is the competitive advantage. The first to know about a funding round gets the meeting.

### NUMI — Design Outsourcing for YC/a16z Startups
- **Problem:** NUMI needed to find early-stage startups looking to outsource their design function. Relied on ~15 hours/week of tab-hopping across LinkedIn, X, investor blogs, and job boards — still missed nuance.
- **Solution:** Deployed listeners for YC batch acceptances and general funding announcements. Monitored founder/investor posts on X/LinkedIn, YC launch pages, investor/VC sites, and press releases. Tracked design pain-point indicators: hiring for product/design functions, rebrands, bursts in design hiring.
- **Result:** Surfaced funding and YC announcements before they hit static databases. Natural language research questions doubled reply rates.
- **Key takeaway:** Signal monitoring + research-based qualification replaces manual prospecting at 10x the coverage.

### Fini — B2C Companies Revamping CX with AI
- **Problem:** Fini (AI customer support) needed to find B2C companies actively revamping their CX teams to enhance support with AI.
- **Solution:** Auto-surfaces B2C companies showing CX team changes, support tool evaluations, and AI adoption signals.
- **Key takeaway:** The signal isn't "uses Zendesk" (static trait). The signal is "revamping CX team AND evaluating AI support tools" (dynamic activity).

## The Search Engine for GTM

OpenFunnel positions itself as "The Search Engine for GTM" — a time-aware search engine for insights and activities in your target segment that reveal pain.

### What "Search Engine" Means in GTM Context

Traditional GTM tools are databases — you query by filters and get lists. A search engine is fundamentally different:
- **Databases answer:** "Give me all companies with 100-500 employees in SaaS" → static list
- **Search engines answer:** "Find companies looking to implement usage-based billing" → inferred pain, with evidence

### Natural Language Search for Pain
- Users describe pain-point activities in plain English, not SQL filters.
- The platform uses reasoning agents (not keyword matching) to interpret intent and find matching companies.
- Example searches:
  - "Find companies looking to implement usage-based billing"
  - "Find companies that moved from OpenAI to self-hosted models in the last 3-6 months"
  - "Find companies who updated their pricing page to introduce an enterprise plan in the last 3 months"
  - "Companies migrating from VM-based deploys to Kubernetes"

### Agents Don't Think in Filters
- People and Company APIs are still CRUD operations not built for GTM search or insight discovery.
- Agents think in meaning, combining world knowledge with user context to craft semantic queries based on intent, not keywords.
- The agent-native data layer uses vector embeddings and semantic matching for company activities.
- The next generation of AI agents doesn't just query data — it reasons with it. Most data layers are built for filters, not meaning.

### One-Shot Intelligence
- The dream: you ask a question in natural language and instantly get meaningful insights from across the internet. No setup, no prompts, no friction.
- Nothing can get lost in translation — building the one-shot AI experience where the user's intent maps directly to the result.
