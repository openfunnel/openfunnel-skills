# Key Principles, B2B Buying & Metrics


## Key GTM Principles & Mental Models

### Spray and Pray is Dead
Mass outbound without signal qualification is negative-ROI. Micro-audiences with context convert.

### Timing > Targeting > Personalization
1. Show up at the right time (buying window)
2. Talk to the right person (people-first)
3. Say the right thing (evidence-based messaging)

### Start with the Signal, Not the List
Discovery should be triggered by activities, not by filtering databases.

### Signals Are Perishable
A signal from 6 months ago is not a signal. Temporal relevance is everything. Leading signals depreciate fast — act when they're fresh.

### Every Signal Has a Person
Account-level intelligence is only actionable when connected to the right person at that account.

### The Best GTM Is Invisible to the Prospect
When outreach is perfectly timed and relevant, it doesn't feel like outbound — it feels like a helpful introduction. "Let your prospects thank you for reaching out to them."

### Right People + Right Company = Commoditized. Right Timing = Differentiation
Every database has company and people data. Nobody sells timing. This is the only defensible moat.

### CRM Is for Storage, Intelligence Is for Action
Don't try to make the CRM smart. Feed it with intelligence from systems designed for discovery and analysis.

### Self-Sourcing Is Rising
The best reps don't wait for marketing to hand them leads. They use signal tools to self-source pipeline. Orgs that enable this win.

### Revenue Comes from Pipeline, Pipeline Comes from Signals
"Pipeline coverage" and "pipeline creation" are the metrics that matter. Signals feed the top of the funnel with qualified, timed opportunities.

## Blind Spots in GTM

### Common GTM Blind Spots

- **Account exhaustion:** Teams working the same accounts repeatedly because they lack new signal-qualified accounts to pursue.
- **Signal confusion:** Teams don't understand why they should deploy unrelated signals or keep them running — they can't see the cross-signal patterns that create buying windows.
- **Operationalization gaps:** Having signals but no established process for converting them to action. The signal-to-meeting pipeline breaks down at operationalization, not detection.
- **Timing blindness:** "Issues with timing in GTM" — build-outs need to be captured with high precision. Buying works without hiring too. Not capturing all buying paths = missing opportunities.
- **Internal vs. external signals:** Over-reliance on internal signals (website visits, product usage, email opens) while ignoring external signals (hiring, social activity, competitive moves) that are often stronger indicators of intent.
- **Revenue stagnation from signal gaps:** "Stagnant revenue — revenue has to keep moving." When signal sources dry up or aren't refreshed, pipeline creation stalls.
- **The six-signal commoditization:** Basic signals (funding, hiring, tech stack, etc.) are commoditized and priced below differentiation threshold. The only real differentiator is buying windows (right timing), which no one else sells.

## How B2B Buying Actually Works

### The Buying Reality

- Buyers buy when there is a sense of organizational need — need often emerges in time, not in a single moment.
- The best leads come from conversations where the prospect reveals pain. Signal intelligence replicates this discovery at scale.
- "How GTM org and enterprise buying is structured" — buying involves multiple stakeholders, budget cycles, and evaluation processes.
- The buying process is rarely linear: need emerges → internal alignment → vendor research → evaluation → decision. Signals can indicate each phase.

### Who Buys, Who Uses

The buyer varies by org structure:
- **Sales-led orgs:** Sales/BDR leader buys and runs signal intelligence.
- **Marketing/Demand Gen-led orgs:** DG/Marketing buys and runs it (BDRs use it daily).
- **Full-cycle AE orgs:** AE leader buys/uses.
- Sometimes marketing doesn't own pipeline — they're only involved in creatives.
- Whoever owns pipeline is the natural buyer + primary user.

### The Champion Problem

Two common buyer reactions that kill deals:
1. "Cool... now what do I actually do with this every day?" — Power without workflow. No clear output, no default motion, no trialable loop.
2. "Very interesting, but I can't get people to sign off — what's it moving? Why now?" — They get it but can't sell internally. No metric, no before/after, no ROI hypothesis.

Both stem from the same root: pitching a system, not an outcome. The product gets consumed in the laziest way (pull a list, run a sequence) or doesn't get bought at all.

**Solution:** The trial must answer both objections: reach out using signal-qualified people → measure reply rates, meetings booked, CTR vs. baseline → within 14-30 days show concrete conversion improvement. That's the "what's it moving" answer the champion puts in front of their VP.

## Metrics That Matter

### Signal Quality Metrics
- Signal-to-meeting conversion rate
- Signal-to-pipeline conversion rate
- Average signals per converted account
- Signal freshness (time from detection to action)

### Operational Metrics
- Accounts discovered per day/week
- People enriched per signal
- Campaign creation rate
- CRM sync accuracy and freshness

### Business Impact Metrics
- Pipeline created from signal-sourced accounts
- Revenue attributed to signal-sourced pipeline
- Positive reply rate on signal-based outreach vs. cold outreach
- Time-to-first-meeting for signal-sourced vs. list-sourced accounts
