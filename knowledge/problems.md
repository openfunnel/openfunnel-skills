# The Core Problems & Activity-First GTM


## The Four Core Problems

The B2B GTM stack has been built on a lie: that having a list of companies matching firmographic filters equals pipeline. OpenFunnel identifies four interlocking problems:

### Problem 1: Static Account Lists Don't Work
- Tools like Apollo, ZoomInfo provide static snapshots — outdated filters disconnected from real buying behavior.
- "B2B SaaS, 501–1000 employees, uses Salesforce" is targeted spam, not targeting. No insights, no buying signals, no understanding of pain.
- Static databases lead to wasted credits on inactive accounts. Before OpenFunnel, teams relied on static databases and couldn't segment accounts by live activity.
- Growing markets and emerging companies aren't even in these databases.
- **OpenFunnel's answer: Dynamic Account Discovery from Live Market Insights** — automatically sourcing only accounts that have live signals, not static lists.

### Problem 2: Commoditized Signals
- Every competitor has access to the same database. Everyone is spamming the same channels with the same data.
- Generic signals (job changes, funding rounds, website visits) are becoming commoditized — everyone sees the same data from the same providers.
- One-time snapshots have no timing, no triggers for when pain surfaces.
- **OpenFunnel's answer: Domain-Specific Market Intelligence** — verticalized, custom signals defined in natural language, not off-the-shelf triggers everyone else uses.

### Problem 3: 3rd-Party Blackbox Intent Data Is a Scam
- Traditional intent data (Bombora, 6sense) is a black box — keyword surge scores with no transparency into what actually happened.
- Intent scores with no evidence, no source URLs, no reason codes. You can't verify what triggered the score.
- Creates an industry-wide loop where nobody admits it's failing because everyone bought into the same metric.
- **OpenFunnel's answer: Every Insight Is Inferred & Contextualized with Full Context** — evidence URLs, reason codes, and evidence snippets for every signal. Explainable, not opaque.

### Problem 4: Minimal Real-Time Signal Coverage
- Most tools cover a narrow set of signal types — funding, job changes, maybe website visits. That's <0.02% of observable activity.
- The real signals are scattered across LinkedIn, X, investor blogs, job boards, GitHub, Discourse, company blogs, press releases, regulatory filings.
- **OpenFunnel's answer: 99.98% Real-Time Signal Coverage** — agentic workflows that monitor any web source, not just API-accessible databases.

**The fundamental insight:** Just because your ICP exists in a database doesn't mean they need your solution right now. ICP match ≠ buying intent.

## Activity-First GTM

The paradigm shift from "list-first" to "activity-first" — discovery should start from observable activities and signals, not from static company lists.

**Core principle:** Start with the Signal, not with a List.

- Rather than basic firmographic filters like "AI companies" or "uses Kubernetes," activity-first GTM reveals active investment patterns and team expansion tied to specific infrastructure scaling.
- "VP Infra team interaction with competitor content + specialist hiring" outperforms simple "uses AWS" targeting.
- Activities are observable, timestamped, and verifiable — they come with evidence URLs and reason codes.
- Teams using activity-based hooks achieve 2.8x higher positive-reply rates than generic signal approaches.

**How it changes the workflow:**
1. Define what activities indicate pain/need (signals)
2. Agents autonomously monitor the internet for those activities 24/7
3. When activity is detected → enrich account → find right people → surface with context
4. Rep acts on signal-rich, time-relevant prospects instead of cold lists
