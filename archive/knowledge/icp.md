# ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

An ICP is a definition of who your ideal customer is. It tells you WHO to target.

**ICP = Firmographic Fit + Trait Fit + Qualifiers**

## Firmographic Fit (Who could buy)
The table-stakes filter. Industry, employee count, funding stage, revenue range, geography.
- "B2B SaaS companies, 100–1000 employees, Series A+, US-based"
- This is what every database sells. Narrows the universe but tells you nothing about timing or need.
- Firmographic fit alone produces massive lists where 95%+ have zero interest right now.

## Trait Fit (Who should buy)
A natural language description of what the company does or who they are — beyond what firmographic checkboxes capture.
- "AI-native ERP company", "developer tools for financial services", "vertical SaaS for logistics"
- Traits describe what a company IS. They change slowly.
- Traits are searchable — describe what you're looking for, get instant results via vector similarity.

## Qualifiers (Who actually fits)
Specific attributes that sharpen fit beyond traits. The "yes, but do they actually..." questions.
- **Technographics** — uses Kubernetes, runs on AWS, has Snowflake in their stack
- **Team composition** — has 5+ support engineers, just hired a CISO, no dedicated data team
- **Product signals** — launched an API, has a self-serve tier, ships a mobile app
- **Operational markers** — SOC2 certified, HIPAA compliant, ISO 27001

Qualifiers turn a broad trait match into a precise one. "AI-native ERP company" is a trait. "AI-native ERP company with 50+ support engineers and no dedicated DevOps team" is a qualified match — and a much better indication they need your help.

## ICP is static

An ICP tells you WHO. It doesn't tell you WHEN or WHY NOW. That's the gap — see [Live ICP](live_icp.md) for the temporal layer that turns a static ICP into a timed, actionable one.
